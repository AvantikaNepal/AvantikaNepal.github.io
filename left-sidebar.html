Exploring Technology, Data, and Human Impact

Over the past few years, my work has focused on the intersection of artificial intelligence, data visualization, and policy research, with a particular interest in human-computer interaction (HCI) and machine learning. I am passionate about creating tools that transform complex data into actionable insights, making technology both accessible and meaningful.

My research and projects are driven by curiosity about how AI can simplify decision-making, reveal hidden patterns, and empower users to interact with data more intuitively. I am particularly interested in applying these technologies to policy analysis, human behavior understanding, and data-driven solutions that have real-world impact, especially in developing contexts.

I’ve also authored a research article on Action and Facial Recognition for Transcribing Human Gestures with LSTM , currently under review for publication. My work reflects a blend of technical rigor, creativity, and purpose, and I aim to develop AI tools that enhance understanding, support decision-making, and bridge the gap between humans and machines.

I believe that good technology doesn’t just automate, it illuminates. My goal is to design intelligent systems that help people see patterns, make better decisions, and bridge the gap between humans and machines.

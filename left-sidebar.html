Exploring Technology, Data, and Human Impact

Over the past few years, my work has focused on the intersection of artificial intelligence, data visualization, and policy research. Each project reflects my curiosity about how technology can simplify complex issues — from visualizing policy documents using AI, to recognizing human expressions through machine learning models.

My projects, including VisionMesh and AI Policy Visualizer, are not just experiments in code but steps toward building systems that bridge the gap between data and decision-making. I’m passionate about creating AI-driven tools that are accessible, ethical, and impactful particularly for developing contexts where technology can make the biggest difference.

I’ve always been fascinated by how technology can turn abstract ideas into tangible insights. From collecting my own datasets for sign language recognition to developing tools that visualize policy information, my projects reflect both creativity and purpose.

I believe that good technology doesn’t just automate, it illuminates. My goal is to design intelligent systems that help people see patterns, make better decisions, and bridge the gap between humans and machines.

I have also authored a research article on Action and Facial Recognition To Transcribe Human Gestures With LSTM Deep Learning Architecture , currently under review for publication.
